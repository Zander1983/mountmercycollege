About Us

Mount Mercy College was founded in 1965 under the trusteeship of the Sisters of Mercy.

Mission Statement

In Mount Mercy College, we strive to promote self worth and self confidence in a Christian environment. We aim to enable students to develop their full potential with emphasis on the academic, cultural, social, spiritual, aesthetic and technological areas and to foster a sense of caring and justice.

Our Mercy Heritage

Catherine McAuley founded the Religious Congregation of the sisters of Mercy in 1831.

Mercy Philosophy of Education

Inspired by the vision of Catherine McAuley. Mercy education is committed to holistic development and to the achievement of the full potential of each student, particularly those who are disadvantaged or marginalised.

It is a process informed and influenced by the teaching and example of Jesus Christ and is eonducted in an atmosphere of care, respect and joy.

Mercy education is committed to on-going whole-school development in collaboration and partnership with the Board of Management, Staff, Parents and the wider community.

Our Care of The Environment

Our school community must work to maintain our three Green Flag awards

As a student in a Mercy School I am called to:

  1. value my own worth as the child of a loving God
  2. develop and share my gifts and talents to the best of my ability
  3. show respect for all, having regard for the rights of others
  4. be particularly sensitive to those who have special needs
  5. live in harmony with others, knowing how to forgive and to seek forgiveness
  6. be a good team player, co-operative with teachers and other students
  7. appreciate all of creation', caring for the earth and respecting life grow in my understanding of my cultural heritage
  8. respect people of other races, traditions and creeds
  9. learn to be a responsible citizen of Ireland and a conscientious member of the European Community
